Feature Guide

BookHunt

A personal book explorer for the serious reader — search the whole of Google Books, keep a private library with reading status, ratings and notes, and follow AI-surfaced themes from one title to the next.

00

At a glance

BookHunt is a single-page app organized around five connected views. A persistent top bar (Discover · Search · Library, plus a search field and a live library count) is present everywhere, so any book or author is two clicks away. Your library — status, ratings, notes, and curated related reads — is saved locally on your device and persists between visits.

The catalog

A curated core set with rich metadata, extended live by the Google Books API so a search reaches virtually any title in print.

Your library

Books you’ve added, each with a reading status, a personal rating, freeform notes, and your own related-read picks.

01

Discover

home

The landing view and browsing home. It introduces the catalog through curated shelves and recommendations drawn from what’s already on your shelves.

Purpose

Open-ended browsing and re-discovery — a place to start when you don’t have a specific title in mind.

User actions
  • Search from the hero or top bar
  • Open any book or author
  • Jump to recommended reads based on your library
  • Click the status chart to jump into the filtered Library
02

Search

results

The find-and-filter view. It runs a local catalog search and an AI-assisted search that interprets natural-language and thematic queries — then, in parallel, queries Google Books so results aren’t limited to the curated catalog.

Purpose

Locate any specific book, or surface every title that fits a subject, mood, or theme.

User actions
  • Type a query, or arrive by tapping a theme or mood
  • Filter by status, subject, mood and century
  • Open any result’s book or author page

↳ Catalog matches list first, followed by a “From Google Books” section. Google results are first-class — each opens a full detail page and can be added to your library. Tapping a theme or mood pill on any book page also lands here.

03

Book detail

book

The heart of the app. Everything about one title, plus every action you can take on it.

What it shows
  • Cover, title, author, average rating, year
  • My rating — your own interactive star rating
  • Pages and an estimated time to read
  • Specifications: category, publisher, language, ISBN-13
  • A link out to the Google Books page
  • Themes — AI-generated, clickable to find more
  • Mood pills — clickable to find books that feel alike
  • Reading status badge (queued / reading / finished / abandoned)
  • AI summary, your notes, and reader reviews (tabbed)
  • More by the same author
  • Related reads — library-aware and curated
User actions
  • Add / remove from library
  • Set reading status and your own rating
  • Write and save notes
  • Tap a theme or mood to find related titles
  • Open the author or any related book
Related reads

Combines your hand-picked titles with algorithm suggestions. Books in your library appear in full color and sort first; others are dimmed with a + to add. You can +/– any card to your library inline, and remove only the related books you added.

04

Author

author

A focused profile for one writer.

Purpose

Context on the author and a complete view of their bibliography — catalog titles, or live results from Google Books for an external author.

User actions
  • Read the author’s biography
  • Open any of their books
  • Return to the previous page
05

Library

library

Your personal collection — every book you’ve added, organized by reading status, with at-a-glance stats. When empty, it shows an invitation to add your first book rather than empty charts.

Purpose

Track and manage what you’re reading, have finished, plan to read, or set aside.

User actions
  • Browse by status group
  • Click a pie chart (status, subject, author) to filter the shelf
  • Change status or remove a book
  • Open any book or its author
  • Add books by importing from a photo
06

How the pages connect

Navigation is a simple stack — every link pushes a new view and a Back button in the top bar returns from anywhere. The top bar reaches the three hubs at any time; books, authors, themes, moods, and the charts weave the views together.

Discover →search / pick Search →open result Book detail
Book detail →author link Author →their books Book detail
Book detail →tap theme / mood Search →incl. Google Books Book detail
Discover →click status chart Library →filtered shelf Book detail
Book detail →add to library Library →open / manage Book detail
Book detail ↔related reads Book detail

The connective tissue: author names always link to the author page; every book reference (results, related reads, bibliography, recommendations) opens that book’s page; theme and mood pills route into Search; the status pie on Discover and every chart in the Library filter or open the Library; and adding a book anywhere updates the Library count in the top bar.

07

Running the front-end

The export is a static front-end: open BookHunt.html through any web server (the .jsx files are compiled in the browser). Google Books search calls the public API directly from the browser — keyless, but rate-limited per IP, so it falls back to a friendly notice if briefly throttled. The AI features — summaries, theme generation, and AI search — call a model at runtime; without that endpoint they fall back to curated content built into data.jsx, so the app stays fully usable either way.

  • BookHunt.html — entry point & script loader
  • styles.css — the warm literary design system
  • data.jsx · lib.jsx — catalog data and logic (search, Google Books, recommendations, themes)
  • app.jsx — shell, routing, library state
  • components.jsx — shared UI (covers, cards, stars, badges)
  • screens-main · screens-detail · screens-library — the five views
  • tweaks-panel.jsx — in-app appearance controls
